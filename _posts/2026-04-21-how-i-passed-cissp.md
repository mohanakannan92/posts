# Stage 1: Free Run

I Gathered all required resource
I Gathered all required resource
- Subscribed to infosec4tc’s CISSP, CISM and CISA bundle package
- Sybex Official study guide (OSG)

- Just read complete book page by page once, answered all question at back of each chapter where I scored Avg 45% very far from to pass percentage.
- I read that book again this time writing down small summary of paragraph which I just read on same page in OSG, underlining the important points, new info and new jargons.
- Read sunflower document
- Took domain based practice test from infosec4tc I scored 60–65%, still not close to pass percentage which was set to 80%
- Took Shone Harris 5th edition practice question and Sybex official practice test domain wise question book I scored around 45% in Shone Harris and 65% in Sybex Practice question, so conceptual wise i was confident, still need to gain more knowledge and need to work with situation related questions.

Almost 3 months over!!

Feelings: Likely doubted on my preparation, likely imposter syndrome, so I wanted to verify my learning practice/approach to this CISSP and would like to know where I stand among other aspirants whether I'm doing it correctly or not.

## Stage 2: Buckled up and fasten laces :)

Watched multiple sourced YouTube videos especially guidance video from 
Prabh Nair
, reading CISSP blog, talked with people who completed CISSP and their experience, comments, @Luke Ahmed “https://www.studynotesandtheory.com/” (SNT) was helpful, gather all info related to preparation, approach, purpose and I create my own Chart/Progress Tracker/Path and i was “Military Strict” :) to follow that. I had healthy schedule to sit everyday, that’s where real game started !!! should have done that before!.

NOTE: Taking notes was very time consuming and painful when you do after 5 years of completing engineering degree.

- All started.. taking printout of often forgetting topics, sticking mind maps in cupboard, doors, walls trying to recollect whenever I was ideal and trying to relate learned topics with everyday real-life usage(this was interesting phase) it is cool to see who it is used.

Mantra: understand the topic, not to memories everything's, forgetting is natural, apply 5w’s rule Who, What, When Where, and Why for each topic whichever is applicable

- Took Subscription “Studynotesandtheory”, gone through all videos, took practice question completed A-Z, very tough practice questions which never allowed me to pass at all, haha, :) everyday i took 25 question scored 55–60% in 2–3 hr(reading the answer, stared taking hand written notes and understanding them).
- Gone through YouTube “IT dojo” complete videos, stared taking hand written notes here too!
- subscribed to “official ISC2 CISSP app”, went through all questions (domain wise and practice questions, etc.), I was really good one, I took notes!, it will help you to keep all topic in finger tips, it will make you feel confident enough with conceptual wise.

- By the time it was almost 7–8 months now, again took infosec4tc questions, Shone Harris questions 5th edition and OSG back question, I was consistently scoring 65–70%, that gave me satisfaction, effort I put really worked I know we need to score >85% in practice question to be safer side, though I was clearing, some what better than before.

- Once again went through Sybex OSG, just revision of those underlined items and important points
- 11th hour CISSP- went through twice
- SNT flashcard
- Memory palace- last time revised twice
- Total tester- this was again difficult practice question, managed to get score 70–72%

- Sunflower 2 version(went through 3 times)
- Think like manager — Good book, help you understand the key and expectation from CISSP exam.
- Especially ISC2 official CISSP app, which help me to learn conceptual wise this was enough for me to manage situation related question in Exam.
- Prabh Nair coffee shots
- My notes (important)
